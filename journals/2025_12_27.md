	- to get **acquainted** with someone means {{cloze to get to know them better}}
		- When getting **acquainted** with someone, there is much to consider.
		- Having dinner with someone is a good way to get **acquainted**.
		- Nice to meet you. Let's talk to get **acquainted**.
	- an **acquaintance** is {{cloze someone that you know, but may not know well}}
		- We are old **acquaintances**. We used to work together.
	- a **companion** is {{cloze someone or something that you spend a lot of time with}}
		- She is a great **companion**. We do everything together.
		- Who needs a boyfriend? My dog is a great **companion**.
		- My dogs and cats are my **companions**.
	- to be **beneficial** is {{cloze to help or have a good affect on something}}
		- Good relationships are hard work because they must be **beneficial** for both people.
		- The help you gave me on my paper was very **beneficial**.
		- It is **beneficial** for her to cook and him to clean.
	- to be **on the rocks** means {{cloze to be having an unpleasant time in a romantic relationship}}
		- “on the rocks”的意思是在恋爱关系中过得不愉快。
		- Our relationship is **on the rocks** right now, and I just don't know what to do.
		- It looks like their relationship is really **on the rocks**.
